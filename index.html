Intent on pursuing Game Design, Christian later found that Los Medanos College did not offer any academic program to acquire a degree and game design, and instead registered under the fine arts that he aimlessly endured for four years. After four years, Christian later found that the neighboring Diablo Valley College (DVC) under the same district (Contra Costa Community College District) did offer an academic program to pursue game design, which counselors just so happen to never mention in the years he was attending Los Medanos College. Christian immediately went to work on an Animation and Game Design degree after he had started attending Diablo Valley College. Within two years, Christian completed all the requirements for the Animation and Game Design Degree as well as nearly all curriculum required to transfer to a University of California. By 2019, after spending approximately six years as a full-time student pursuing a two-year degree at two different community colleges and one college extension. Christian was finally accepted to one of the very few UCs that had a game design program, UC Santa Cruz. Once again, he packed his belongings and moved to the ghettos of Antioch, California. Even with UCSC efforts, Christian compiled files and emails for a lawsuit against them and is still ahead on his requirements, even though UCSC has partially succeeded in pushing his intended completion three months back, he is still ahead by three months (originally six). Currently, he is fighting a penalty from UCSC that claims that unless given circumstances, he must provide evidence that he is a part-time student, else face a full-tuition bill that a full-time student normally has.
